# ACHILLES PROTOCOLS

## 1. HIERARCHY OF ACCURACY
- **Inaccurate (Worst):** Provably false. Contradicted by reputable evidence.
- **Unsupported:** Impossible to find any reputable evidence for or against.
- **Disputed:** Expert disagreement.
- **Accurate:** All claims supported by evidence or widely accepted opinions.

## 2. HOW TO HEDGE
A hedge provides info but does NOT directly fulfill the user's request.
- *Hedge:* "I don't know your location, but here are bars in NYC..."
- *Not a Hedge:* "You cannot pet a unicorn, they are not real."

## 3. SEVERITY AXIS (1-5)
If an answer is NOT accurate, evaluate the *most severe* flaw based on:
- **Centrality:** Was the flaw central to the prompt?
- **Obviousness:** Would a 12-year-old instantly know it's fake?
- **Measurable/Distance:** Is it wildly off numerically (e.g. 1950 vs 1969)?
- **Sensitivity:** Politics, religion, nations?
- **YMYL:** Health or money?

## 4. LINKS = CLAIMS
- You MUST click links! A 404 broken link = Inaccurate claim!
- Ignore `[† Source]` and image source links. 
- Ignore "googleusercontent.com" links.

## 5. REPUTABLE SOURCES ONLY
- ✅ Wikipedia, CDC, WHO, News, Books.
- 🛑 NO LLMs, NO Reddit/Quora, NO "AI Overview", NO Social Media!

## 6. COMMENTS
Must explain:
1. Fact-check sources (URLs) used. 
2. Clear rationale for the comparative rating (why A over B).
3. Severity reasoning if there are flaws.
